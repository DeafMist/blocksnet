masterplan_tools.method.blocks.blocks_cutter module#

This module is aimed to cut the geometry of the specified city into city blocks. Blocks are close to cadastral unit, however in the way they are calculated in this method they become suitable to be used in masterplanning process.

get_blocks() → GeoDataFrame[source]#

Main method.

This method gets city’s boundaries from OSM. Then iteratively water, roads and railways are cutted from city’s geometry. After splitting city into blocks invalid blocks with bad geometries are removed.

For big city it takes about ~ 1-2 hours to split big city’s geometry into thousands of blocks. It takes so long because splitting one big geometry (like city) by another big geometry (like roads or waterways) is computationally expensive. Splitting city by roads and water entities are two the most time consuming processes in this method.

For cities over the Russia it could be better to upload water geometries from elsewhere, than taking them from OSM. Somehow they are poorly documented on OSM.

Returns:
blocks

a GeoDataFrame of city blocks
